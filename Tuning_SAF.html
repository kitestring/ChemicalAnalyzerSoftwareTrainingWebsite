Source and Analyzer Focus

A Source and Analyzer Focus (SAF) adjusts the flight tube voltages in order to optimize the intensity, resolution, and symmetry of the spectral peaks produced by the instrument. This is an automatic tune; no user intervention is required. The following screenshot is an example of what the user might see on the screen during a SAF. Note, this window can be minimized, but cannot be closed while the system is activley acquiring a SAF.

Source and Analyzer Focus Stage Tracking Viewer.

In general, users can ignore the information that is displayed here—the most useful information is how many stages are complete and how many are left. “Details” displays advanced information about the status of the tune. “Track active stage” means the tab for each stage will be automatically activated when its stage begins. “Export data” allows you to export the raw data collected during the tune in CSV format

A SAF can be added to the Acquisition Queue either as part of an Auto-Tune or individually as a single line. There are two videos below, the first demonstrates how to add a SAF to the Acquisition Queue individually, and the second shows what a SAF looks like as it is being acquired.
